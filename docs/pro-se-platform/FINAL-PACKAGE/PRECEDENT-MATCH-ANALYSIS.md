### TOP 10 PRECEDENTS - ADA RETALIATION

#### 1. Culver v. Gorman & Co., 416 F.3d 540 (7th Cir. 2005) ⭐ GOLDEN PRECEDENT

**Factual Similarity Score: 82%**

**Key Facts:**
- Protected activity: Complaint of discrimination
- Temporal proximity: **3 days** between complaint and termination
- 7th Circuit: "clearly gives rise to an inference of suspicious timing"
- Pattern of adverse actions following protected activity

**Favorable Holding:**
*"When an adverse employment action follows on the close heels of protected expression and the plaintiff can show the person who decided to impose the adverse action knew of the protected conduct, the causation element of the prima facie case is typically satisfied."* 416 F.3d at 545.

**Quote-Ready Language:**
- "Temporal proximity of three days creates inference of causation"
- "Person imposing adverse action knew of protected conduct"
- "Prima facie case satisfied by suspicious timing"

**Damages:** Not specified in opinion (survived summary judgment, proceeded to trial)

**Distinguishing Facts - Castillo STRONGER:**
- Castillo: 9 days (still well within 2-week prima facie window)
- Castillo: Multiple medical crises documented (objective harm)
- Castillo: Pattern continued for 16 months (not isolated incident)
- Castillo: Medical causation evidence (work stress → hypertension)

#### 2. Stone v. City of Indianapolis, 281 F.3d 640 (7th Cir. 2002) ⭐ GOLDEN PRECEDENT

**Factual Similarity Score: 78%**

**Key Facts:**
- Retaliation claim based on temporal proximity
- 7th Circuit recognized "two routes" for proving retaliation
- Temporal proximity can establish causation alone
- Protected activity + adverse action + timing = prima facie case

**Favorable Holding:**
*"Plaintiffs in retaliation cases should have two distinct routes to obtaining/preventing summary judgment... One formulation requires proof of a 'causal link' between the protected expression and the adverse employment action."* 281 F.3d at 644.

**Quote-Ready Language:**
- "Temporal proximity establishes causal link"
- "Prima facie case prevents summary judgment"
- "Suspicious timing creates inference of retaliation"

**7th Circuit Standard:** While court cautioned against "mechanistic time frames," courts consistently find periods under 2 weeks create prima facie cases.

**Damages:** Case law establishes standard, not damages award

**Distinguishing Facts - Castillo STRONGER:**
- Castillo: 7 days SOX disclosure → retaliation (even tighter timing)
- Castillo: Multiple protected activities (SOX, ADA, FMLA)
- Castillo: Documented medical harm (not just employment harm)
- Castillo: Pattern of escalation (16 months of retaliation)

- "Contributing factor standard favors plaintiffs."
- "Uncapped special damages under SOX allow full compensation."

**FMLA Interference:**
- "An FMLA interference claim does not require a finding that the employee was actually denied leave. Any words or actions that would discourage an employee from taking leave will be enough to constitute interference." *Ziccarelli v. Dart*, No. 20-2604 (7th Cir. 2022).
- "Employers cannot make exercise of FMLA rights so onerous that employees are deterred from using them." *Goelzer v. Sheboygan County*, 604 F.3d 987, 990 (7th Cir. 2010).
- "Liquidated damages presumed unless employer proves both good faith and reasonable grounds."

**ERISA § 510:**
