# Zordic Music Studio - High School Music Production Curriculum

## Overview

This comprehensive 10-week curriculum teaches high school students (ages 14-18) music production through the Zordic Music Studio framework. Using agent-assisted, project-based learning, students progress from sound fundamentals to advanced production techniques.

## Curriculum Structure

### ðŸ“š Five Major Units

1. **Fundamentals (Weeks 1-2)**: Sound waves, rhythm, music theory, timbre
2. **Beat Making (Weeks 3-4)**: Drum patterns, layering, grooves, sampling
3. **Melody Creation (Weeks 5-6)**: Scales, composition, synthesis, lead/bass
4. **Harmony & Arrangement (Weeks 7-8)**: Chord progressions, song structure, mixing
5. **Advanced Techniques (Weeks 9-10)**: Effects, automation, genre-specific production, collaboration

### ðŸŽ¯ Learning Outcomes

By the end of this curriculum, students will be able to:

- Understand the physics of sound and digital audio
- Program professional-quality drum patterns and beats
- Compose original melodies using music theory principles
- Create harmonic progressions and arrange complete songs
- Apply advanced production techniques (effects, automation, mixing)
- Produce music in multiple genres
- Collaborate effectively with other producers
- Present and discuss their creative work professionally

## Quick Start Guide

### For Teachers

1. **Review the curriculum:**
   - Read `music-production-curriculum.json` for complete scope and sequence
   - Study `lesson-plans.md` for detailed teaching instructions
   - Familiarize yourself with `assessment-rubrics.md` for grading

2. **Prepare your classroom:**
   - Ensure each student has access to Zordic Music Studio
   - Set up audio equipment (headphones required, monitors optional)
   - Configure agent support features in Zordic
   - Organize file storage and naming conventions

3. **Week 1 Setup:**
   - Introduce Zordic interface
   - Establish classroom norms (headphone protocol, sharing work)
   - Begin Lesson 1: Introduction to Sound Waves
   - Assign first creative challenge

4. **Ongoing:**
   - Follow lesson plans for each 90-minute session
   - Use agents to provide real-time student support
   - Collect formative assessments during exercises
   - Grade creative challenges using provided rubrics
   - Facilitate unit projects at end of each unit

### For Students

1. **Get familiar with Zordic:**
   - Explore the interface
   - Meet the agent assistants (theory-agent, melody-agent, beat-agent, etc.)
   - Practice saving projects with proper naming

2. **Each lesson you will:**
   - Learn new concepts through agent-assisted tutorials
   - Practice with interactive exercises
   - Complete a creative challenge to apply your skills
   - Build toward a unit project

3. **Projects you'll create:**
   - Week 2: Sonic foundation portfolio
   - Week 4: Simple beat production
   - Week 6: Melodic composition
   - Week 8: Full song production
   - Week 10: Advanced production portfolio
   - Final: Curated showcase portfolio

## Learning Approach

### Agent-Assisted Learning

This curriculum integrates 12 specialized AI agents:

- **theory-agent**: Music theory instruction
- **melody-agent**: Melodic composition support
- **rhythm-agent**: Rhythm and timing guidance
- **beat-agent**: Drum programming assistance
- **harmony-agent**: Chord progressions and harmony
- **synth-agent**: Synthesis and sound design
- **mix-agent**: Mixing and audio processing
- **arrangement-agent**: Song structure and arrangement
- **analysis-agent**: Audio and music analysis
- **production-agent**: Overall production workflow
- **collaboration-agent**: Team workflow support
- **genre-agent**: Genre-specific techniques

**Agents provide:**
- Real-time feedback during exercises
- Adaptive difficulty based on performance
- Interactive tutorials with visualizations
- Creative suggestions while respecting student vision
- Technical assistance on-demand

### Project-Based Learning

Students learn by creating authentic music production projects:

1. **Creative challenges** (end of each lesson): Quick applications of new skills
2. **Unit projects** (end of each 2-week unit): Comprehensive demonstrations of mastery
3. **Final portfolio** (weeks 11-12): Curated showcase of best work

### Progressive Difficulty

- **Beginner** (Weeks 1-2): Fundamentals, tool familiarity
- **Beginner-Intermediate** (Weeks 3-4): Beat making basics
- **Intermediate** (Weeks 5-6): Melodic and harmonic composition
- **Intermediate-Advanced** (Weeks 7-8): Complete song production
- **Advanced** (Weeks 9-10): Professional techniques and genre mastery

## Assessment Framework

### Grade Breakdown

- **Formative Assessments (30%)**: Daily exercises, participation, practice
- **Creative Challenges (20%)**: 20 lesson-end challenges
- **Unit Projects (30%)**: 5 major projects (6% each)
- **Final Portfolio (20%)**: Curated portfolio with presentation

### Grading Categories

All work assessed across four dimensions:

1. **Technical Proficiency (40%)**: Tool usage, concept application, execution quality
2. **Creative Expression (30%)**: Originality, musical merit, artistic voice
3. **Professional Practice (20%)**: Organization, documentation, communication
4. **Growth & Effort (10%)**: Improvement, feedback response, engagement

### Assessment Tools

- Detailed rubrics for every assignment (see `assessment-rubrics.md`)
- Self-assessment templates for student reflection
- Peer review forms for collaborative learning
- Agent-generated progress reports

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do students need prior music experience?**
A: No! This curriculum is designed for complete beginners.

**Q: What if a student finishes early?**
A: Extension challenges and additional genre exploration provided for advanced students.

**Q: How much time outside of class?**
A: Expect 1-2 hours per week for creative challenges and projects.

**Q: Can this be adapted for middle school?**
A: Yes, with simplified projects and extended timelines. See differentiation section.

**Q: What if Zordic agents aren't working?**
A: All lessons include manual alternatives. Agents enhance but aren't required.

**Q: How do I assess creativity objectively?**
A: Rubrics focus on specific creative behaviors (originality, risk-taking, etc.) rather than taste.

**Q: Can students use their own music in projects?**
A: Absolutely! Encourage personal connection through genre and topic choice.
